Marketing Campaign Analytics Project
## 1. Project Overview

This project analyzes the Marketing Campaign Analytics dataset, which reflects the digital advertising performance of a UK-based online retail business. The dataset contains 9,900 campaign records across multiple social media platforms, including Facebook, Instagram, and Pinterest, providing a comprehensive view of campaign performance across reach metrics (impressions), engagement metrics (likes, shares, comments), financial metrics (spend, CPC), and performance metrics (conversions, conversion value). The analysis aims to evaluate marketing effectiveness, optimize budget allocation, and identify high-performing campaign segments.

## 2. Business Context

The company runs seasonal digital marketing campaigns (e.g., Fall Campaign) using two primary ad formats:

- Collection Ads - Showcase multiple products to increase brand awareness and consideration.

- Discount Ads - Promote sales and limited-time offers to drive immediate purchases.

Target audience: UK consumers who use social media on both desktop (at home or work) and mobile devices (for on-the-go shopping).

Business Objectives:

- Increase online sales revenue

- Optimize marketing budget allocation across channels and cities

- Improve brand engagement (likes, shares, comments)

- Identify the most effective channel, device, and ad format

- Scale high-performing campaigns

## 3. Dataset Structure

Total Records: 9,900

Total Fields: 19 (including campaign information, channel, device, location, performance metrics, and financial metrics)

Time Coverage: Multi-period data at daily granularity

Geographical Scope: Multiple cities across the UK, including detailed coordinates (latitude, longitude)

Advertising Channels: Facebook, Instagram, Pinterest

Devices: Desktop, Mobile

## 4. Key Analytical Questions

Q1. Which channel (Facebook, Instagram, Pinterest) generates the highest ROI? Compare conversion rate and cost per conversion.

Q2. How does campaign performance differ between Desktop and Mobile? Which device delivers a higher conversion rate?

Q3. Which city shows the strongest campaign performance? Conduct a geographic analysis based on conversion rate and spend efficiency.

Q4. What is the relationship between engagement (likes, shares, comments) and conversions? Do ads with higher engagement generate better conversion results?

Q5. Compare the effectiveness of Collection Ads and Discount Ads. Which ad format delivers better results?

Q6. What is the average CTR for each channel? Which campaigns exceed the industry benchmark (0.5%–3%)?

Q7. How does campaign performance change over time? Are there any seasonal patterns or day-of-week effects?

Q8. What is the relationship between advertising spend and campaign performance? Is there evidence of diminishing returns?

Q9. Which combination of Channel + Device + Ad Format generates the highest conversion value per GBP spent?

Q10. Are there any underperforming campaign segments that require optimization? What could be the possible causes?
